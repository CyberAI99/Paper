\begin{enabstract}

    3D light field display technology is a key carrier for next-generation immersive visual experience, and, with its glasses-free stereoscopic perception capability, it shows broad application potential in commercial advertising, medical diagnosis and treatment, film and television entertainment, and related fields. Lighting is a core factor for establishing stereoscopic realism and spatial credibility, and its editing quality directly affects the display quality and immersive experience of 3D light field portraits. However, existing 3D light field portrait lighting editing methods still suffer from major technical bottlenecks, including complex and costly acquisition equipment, insufficient multi-view lighting consistency, and temporal flicker in dynamic scenes, which makes it difficult to satisfy the generation requirements of high-quality static and dynamic light field content.
    
To address these issues, this thesis carries out systematic research on 3D light field face lighting editing and builds a complete technical system spanning static foundational methods to dynamic optimization frameworks. The main research work and contributions are summarized as follows:

Firstly, a key-information-driven 3D light field static face lighting editing method is proposed. To solve lighting inconsistency caused by independent multi-view editing in static scenes and the high cost of dense acquisition equipment, a three-stage framework of "highlight removal-lighting editing-dense viewpoint generation" is designed: portrait highlight regions are accurately located and removed through a dual detection mechanism combining brightness thresholding and diffuse component analysis; a dual-path lighting editing strategy is adopted, which not only supports extracting Spherical Harmonic (SH) coefficients from HDR images for environment relighting, but also enables semantic control of lighting direction and intensity by parsing natural language instructions with a BERT model; based on NeRF volume rendering, 90 dense disparity views within a horizontal range of ±30° are generated to satisfy light field display requirements. Experimental results demonstrate that the proposed method outperforms mainstream methods such as NVPR and LRPI on metrics including FID (33.55) and PSNR (32.06), while requiring only 25.92s for single-frame inference. On a 3D light field display, the vote rate for stereoscopic perception reaches 89.19\%, and the vote rate for lighting consistency reaches 92.29\%.

Secondly, a time-space dual-dimension joint optimization framework for dynamic lighting editing of 3D light field face videos is proposed. To address temporal flicker and multi-view lighting mismatch when extending static methods to video sequences, two core optimization modules are designed: the temporal consistency optimization module extracts stable semantic features through a shared encoder, integrates optical-flow alignment with lighting-geometry feature disentanglement, and applies dual loss constraints of "feature L1 + pixel residual L2" to effectively suppress inter-frame lighting flicker; the multi-view consistency optimization module performs 3D geometric alignment based on camera intrinsics and extrinsics, transforms multi-view lighting constraints into a 3D point-wise lighting observation problem through 3D surface sampling, and realizes physically consistent cross-view lighting expression via a multi-factor aggregation mechanism that combines three weights: visibility, normal angle, and lighting confidence. Experimental verification shows that this framework reduces the temporal stability metric WE to 0.0004 and the multi-view lighting error LE to 0.7922, while achieving a comprehensive subjective score of 4.73, significantly outperforming comparative methods such as PTI and SMFR.

Through theoretical innovation and experimental validation, this thesis effectively alleviates core technical bottlenecks in 3D light field face lighting editing and provides a new technical pathway for generating high-quality static and dynamic light field content. The resulting methods can be applied to glasses-free 3D display, VR/AR, film and television production, and other scenarios, and therefore hold important theoretical significance and engineering value.

\end{enabstract}
